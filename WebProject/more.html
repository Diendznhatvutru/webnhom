Fixed-Term Savings: Key Features and Benefits

  • Flexibility in Transactions: Customers can perform transactions at any branch or transaction office of the bank.
  • Customizable Terms: Deposit terms and interest payment methods can be chosen based on the customer's needs.
  • Stable Interest Rates: Offers fixed interest rates for the deposit term, allowing customers to secure stable income during the term.
  • Loan Collateral: Savings account balance can be used as collateral for loans or other financial purposes.
  • Deposit Amounts: Minimum deposit amount varies depending on the bank's policy.
  • Automatic Renewal: Upon maturity, the savings can be automatically renewed with the same term and updated interest rate unless instructed otherwise.
  • Premature Withdrawals: Early withdrawal is allowed, but interest rates may be lower or calculated at demand deposit rates.

Non-fixed-term Savings: Key Features and Benefits

  • High Flexibility: Customers can withdraw or deposit money at any time without prior notice.
  • Lower Interest Rates: Interest rates are typically lower compared to fixed-term savings accounts due to the high liquidity.
  • Convenient Transactions: Customers can access their savings easily through various channels such as online banking, ATMs, or transaction offices.
  • No Minimum Term: Unlike fixed-term savings, there is no required commitment to a specific term, making it suitable for short-term or emergency funds.
  • Interest Calculation: Interest is usually calculated daily and credited to the account monthly or based on the bank's policy.
  • Ideal for Emergency Funds: This account type is perfect for individuals who want quick access to their funds while still earning some interest.
